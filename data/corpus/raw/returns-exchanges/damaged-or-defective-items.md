# Damaged or defective items

If an item arrives damaged or has a manufacturing defect, we will replace it or issue a full refund at no cost to you. Damage claims are handled separately from standard returns—you will not pay a restocking fee, and the 30-day return window does not strictly apply when something arrives broken or flawed.

## What qualifies as damaged or defective

Damage includes items that arrive broken, cracked, chipped, torn, or visibly crushed during shipping. Defects are manufacturing flaws: a skillet with an uneven cooking surface, a blanket with visible stitching gaps, or a mug with glaze defects that affect use.

Cosmetic imperfections that do not affect function—a minor scuff on the underside of a cutting board, slight color variation in natural materials—fall outside the defect policy. If you are unsure whether an issue qualifies, submit photos and our support team will review.

## Starting a damage claim

Reply to your order confirmation email with the subject line "Damage claim — [your order number]" and include two or three clear photos showing the issue. A wide shot of the full item and a close-up of the damage help us assess quickly.

You do not need to return the item before we respond. In many cases we can approve a replacement or refund based on photos alone, especially for heavy or fragile items where return shipping adds unnecessary cost and handling.

Your order number follows the format FG-NNNNNN and appears in your confirmation email. If you cannot locate it, include the email address used at checkout and an approximate order date.

## What happens next

Our team typically reviews damage claims within one business day. If the claim is approved, you will receive a prepaid return label by email. Once the warehouse confirms receipt, a replacement ships or a full refund posts to your original payment method within 5–7 business days.

If a damaged item was marked final sale, the damage policy still applies—final-sale restrictions cover buyer's remorse, not items that arrive broken. See our guide on **Final sale items** for more on what those restrictions normally cover.

For items where return shipping is impractical (a heavy rug with significant damage, for instance), we may approve a refund without requiring return. That decision is made case by case.

## Tips for a faster resolution

Clear photos make the difference. Show the product label or SKU tag if possible, and photograph the outer shipping box if it arrived visibly crushed. Natural light works better than overhead indoor lighting.

If you are reporting a defect rather than visible damage, describe what is wrong in one or two sentences: "The skillet wobbles on a flat surface" or "The blanket has a 3-inch tear along the hem." Specifics help us route the claim correctly.

Standard returns have a $7.95 restocking fee for opened items, but damage claims do not. If you are unsure whether to file a damage claim or a standard return, visit **How to start a return** for a comparison. You can also reach our support team through the **Contacting customer support** article if you need help deciding.

## FAQ

**Do I need to keep the original packaging?**  
Not required, but keeping the box for a few days after delivery makes it easier to document shipping damage if you discover it later.

**What if I notice a defect two months after delivery?**  
Contact us. Manufacturing defects reported within a reasonable window are usually covered even past 30 days, though we will ask for photos and a description of the issue.

**Can I get a replacement if the item is out of stock?**  
If a replacement is not available, we will issue a full refund. You can choose store credit if you prefer, but we cannot hold inventory or backorder replacements for damage claims.
