# Duplicate orders and billing questions

If you see more than one charge from Fieldstone Goods on your statement, it may be a pending authorization rather than a duplicate order. Authorization holds appear alongside final charges for a few days before your bank removes them. Any billing question that requires refund review or dispute resolution is handled by a human specialist—Ember, our support agent, cannot process these requests directly.

## Understanding authorization holds vs. charges

When you place an order, your bank may show two transactions: a pending authorization and a completed charge. The authorization is a temporary hold that verifies funds are available. It typically drops off your statement within 2–5 business days, depending on your bank. Only one charge will finalize.

If both transactions remain as completed charges after five days, that indicates a potential duplicate order. Check your order confirmation emails—if you received two separate order numbers (format FG-NNNNNN), two orders were placed. If you only have one confirmation email but see two charges, a billing specialist will investigate.

## What to do if you think you were charged twice

First, confirm whether both charges have moved from "pending" to "posted" on your statement. If one is still pending, wait three business days for your bank to clear the authorization.

If both charges are posted and you only intended to place one order, gather your order number and the last four digits of the payment method used. Contact Fieldstone customer support for review. A billing specialist will compare the transaction details against our order records and issue a refund if a duplicate charge occurred. See our guide on contacting customer support for the fastest way to reach the team.

Refunds for confirmed duplicate charges are processed to the original payment method within 5–7 business days of verification.

## Why billing questions require a specialist

Ember, our support agent, can help with order tracking, return initiation, and policy questions. However, billing disputes, duplicate charge reviews, and refund exceptions fall outside what Ember can resolve. These requests require manual review of payment processor records and sometimes coordination with your bank.

When you contact support about a billing issue, your inquiry is routed to a specialist who has access to transaction logs and refund tools. This ensures accurate resolution and protects your payment information.

## FAQ

**Can I cancel an order to avoid a duplicate charge?**  
Orders can only be canceled before they ship. If your order has already entered fulfillment, you will need to return the item once it arrives. The standard 30-day return window applies, and refunds land on your original payment method within 5–7 business days of the warehouse receiving your return.

**What if I see a charge but never received a confirmation email?**  
Check your spam folder first. If you still cannot locate a confirmation, contact support with the charge amount, date, and last four digits of your payment method. A specialist will search for the order and resend your confirmation or investigate the charge.

**How long does a billing dispute take to resolve?**  
Most billing reviews are completed within 3–5 business days. Complex cases involving payment processor records may take up to ten business days. You will receive an email update once the specialist completes their review.
