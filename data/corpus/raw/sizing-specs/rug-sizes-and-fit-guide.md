# Rug sizes and fit guide

The Wool Area Rug, 5x7 (FG-HOM-002) works in bedrooms, dining areas, and small living rooms. This guide shows how the 5×7 footprint fits common layouts so you can plan before it arrives.

## Bedroom placement

A 5×7 rug works as a runner at the foot of a queen or king bed. Position the 7-foot edge parallel to the bed frame, leaving 12 to 18 inches of exposed floor between the rug and the wall. The rug should extend 18 to 24 inches beyond each side of the mattress so your feet land on wool when you stand.

For twin or full beds, center the rug under the lower two-thirds of the bed frame with at least 18 inches extending past the foot. This creates a soft landing without overwhelming a smaller room.

## Living room configurations

In a small living room (10×12 feet or similar), center the rug under your main seating area. Front furniture legs—sofa, chairs, coffee table—should rest on the rug while back legs stay on the floor. This anchors the space without requiring a wall-to-wall fit.

If your room is narrower, float the rug entirely under the coffee table with all seating off the edges. Leave at least 8 inches of clearance between the rug perimeter and the nearest wall or baseboard.

## Dining room use

A 5×7 rug fits a small dining table (36 to 48 inches round or square) with four chairs. The rug should extend at least 24 inches beyond the table edge on all sides so chairs remain on the rug when pulled out. Measure your table diameter or width, add 48 inches, and confirm that total does not exceed 84 inches in either direction.

For rectangular tables longer than 60 inches, the 5×7 footprint will not provide adequate chair clearance. You will need a larger rug.

## Care and material notes

The Wool Area Rug is hand-tufted, so expect slight size variation (within 2 inches) and a 1/2-inch pile height. New rugs may shed lightly for the first few weeks—vacuum on low suction without a beater bar. ## FAQ

**Can I layer the 5×7 rug over wall-to-wall carpet?**  
Yes. Use a thin rug pad (not included) to prevent slipping. The combined pile height may create a trip edge, so ensure the rug lies flat.

**What if my space is between two layout examples?**  
Measure the furniture footprint, add your desired clearance (18 to 24 inches per side), and compare to 60×84 inches. If the numbers are close, the rug will work. If you need more than 6 extra inches in any direction, the fit will feel tight.

**Does the rug come with a pad?**  
No. Rug pads are sold separately and are not part of the Fieldstone catalog at this time.
