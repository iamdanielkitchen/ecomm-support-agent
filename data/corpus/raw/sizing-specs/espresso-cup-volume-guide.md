# Espresso cup volumes and fit

The Espresso Cups (FG-KIT-004) hold 2.5 fluid ounces to the rim and are designed for classic Italian-style espresso service. Each cup fits a double shot with room for crema, sits under most home espresso machines, and uses a demitasse profile rather than a latte or cappuccino shape. This guide covers fill capacity, machine clearance, and why these cups are sized the way they are.

## Volume and standard espresso pulls

Each cup holds 2.5 fl oz (74 ml) when filled to the rim. A standard single espresso pull is about 1 oz, and a double is 2 oz. That leaves roughly half an ounce of headroom for crema and foam. If you pull ristretto shots (shorter extractions around 0.75 oz per pull), the cups accommodate two singles with generous space. For lungo shots (3 oz or more), the cup will overflow — these are not built for Americano-style volumes.

The demitasse profile (narrow body, flared rim) concentrates aroma and keeps the crema intact longer than a wide-mouth cup. If you typically drink milk-based drinks like lattes or flat whites, these cups are too small; they are purpose-built for straight espresso.

## Clearance under home machines

The cups measure 2.25 inches tall (5.7 cm) and 2.5 inches wide at the rim. Most home espresso machines (Breville, Gaggia, Rancilio, De'Longhi models) provide 3 to 4 inches of clearance between the spout and drip tray. These cups fit comfortably under that range. If your machine has an adjustable drip tray, you may need to lower it for the cup to sit flush beneath the portafilter spouts.

Machines with very low group heads or fixed single-spout commercial-style outputs may require you to tilt the cup slightly during extraction. For ultra-compact pod machines with less than 2.5 inches of clearance, these cups will not fit.

## Why demitasse and not latte cups

Demitasse (French for "half cup") is the traditional vessel for espresso. The smaller volume keeps the liquid hot longer and concentrates the aromatics at the rim. Latte cups, by contrast, are 6 to 8 oz and designed for milk dilution. The Espresso Cups ship as a set of four, so you have enough for back-to-back pulls without washing between rounds.

The ceramic is safe for daily dishwasher use — see our guide on ceramic cookware and dishwasher use for care details. The glaze is rated for temperatures up to 450°F, so you can preheat the cups in a warming drawer or on top of the machine without risk of cracking.

## FAQ

**Can I use these for tea or cortado drinks?**  
Yes. The 2.5 oz capacity works for small tea servings or cortados (1:1 espresso and steamed milk). For cappuccinos, you would need a larger cup.

**Do the cups come with saucers?**  
No. The set includes four cups only. Saucers are sold separately and not currently stocked.

**Will these fit in a standard cup warmer tray?**  
Most electric cup warmers accommodate cups up to 3 inches in diameter. These cups measure 2.5 inches at the rim, so they fit standard warming trays without issue.
