# Cutting board dimensions and workflow

The Walnut Cutting Board (FG-KIT-002) measures 18 inches long by 12 inches wide by 1.5 inches thick. It's milled from a single slab of North American black walnut, giving you a stable work surface that fits standard countertops and pairs well with chef's knives from 6 to 10 inches. The single-slab construction means no glue lines and better structural integrity over time.

## Board footprint and counter fit

At 18" × 12", the board occupies about 1.5 square feet of counter space. That's large enough to handle prep work for two to four servings without crowding your knife hand, but compact enough to store upright in a cabinet or lean against a backsplash when not in use. The 1.5-inch thickness keeps the board stable during chopping and prevents warping from moisture exposure on one side.

Most home kitchens have at least 24 inches of continuous counter space near the sink or stove. The board leaves 6 inches of clearance on a standard 24-inch counter section, giving you room for a bowl or plate alongside your prep area. If your counter depth is standard (25 inches), the 12-inch width leaves 13 inches behind the board for small appliances or a utensil crock.

## Pairing with common knife sizes

The 18-inch length gives you a full stroke for slicing and rocking cuts with chef's knives up to 10 inches. A standard 8-inch chef's knife has plenty of runway for dicing onions or mincing herbs without your knuckles hitting the edge of the board. The 12-inch width accommodates crosswise cuts for larger vegetables like bell peppers or summer squash.

For smaller tasks, paring knives and utility knades (3 to 6 inches) work comfortably on one half of the board, leaving the other half clean for plating or gathering prepped ingredients. The walnut surface is dense enough to resist deep scoring from sharp blades but soft enough that it won't dull your edge as quickly as bamboo or plastic.

## Single-slab construction

This board is milled from one continuous piece of walnut, not edge-glued strips. Single-slab boards have no seams where moisture or food particles can collect, making them easier to clean and less prone to splitting along glue lines. The grain runs lengthwise, which adds strength and creates the distinctive dark-brown streaks walnut is known for.

Each board shows slight color variation because we source from individual trees, not factory-dyed stock. See our guide on where our wood comes from for details on how we select and dry the walnut before milling. The natural variation means your board is one of a kind, though dimensions and thickness remain consistent across every unit.

## Care and longevity

Walnut is a hardwood that holds up to daily use when maintained correctly. Hand-wash with warm water and mild soap, then dry immediately. Avoid soaking or running it through the dishwasher, which can cause cracking. Monthly oiling with food-safe mineral oil keeps the surface conditioned and prevents moisture from penetrating the grain. For detailed care instructions, see our guide on cutting board maintenance.

With proper care, the board develops a darker patina over the first year of use. Light knife marks are normal and blend into the grain over time. The 1.5-inch thickness gives you enough material to sand and refinish the surface if deep cuts accumulate, though most home cooks find that unnecessary for the board's usable life.
