# Cast iron skillet capacity guide

The Cast Iron Skillet, 10" (FG-KIT-001) measures 10 inches across the top rim and holds approximately 2 quarts of liquid. It comfortably cooks 2–4 servings depending on the dish, weighs 5.2 pounds, and works on all stovetops and in ovens up to 500°F.

## Cooking capacity by servings

The 10-inch diameter gives you enough cooking surface for most everyday meals. A typical breakfast fits 3–4 eggs or 4–6 strips of bacon without crowding. For pan-seared proteins, you can cook two 6-ounce chicken breasts or a 12-ounce steak with room to flip comfortably. Shallow braises and skillet pastas work well at the 2-quart capacity, serving 3–4 people as a main dish.

If you are cooking for one or two, the skillet handles single portions with plenty of space. For larger gatherings, you may want to cook in batches or use multiple pans.

## Oven and stovetop compatibility

This skillet is oven-safe up to 500°F with no additional components to remove. The single-piece cast iron construction, including the handle, makes it a reliable choice for stovetop-to-oven recipes like cornbread, frittatas, or roasted chicken thighs. It works on gas, electric coil, glass-top, and induction cooktops. On induction, the skillet heats efficiently due to its magnetic cast iron material.

Preheat gradually on medium heat rather than high to avoid hot spots. Cast iron retains heat longer than nonstick, so you may find yourself lowering the burner setting once the pan comes to temperature.

## Weight and handling expectations

At 5.2 pounds, the Cast Iron Skillet, 10" feels substantially heavier than a comparable nonstick pan, which typically weighs 1.5 to 2 pounds. The extra weight comes from the thick cast iron walls that provide even heat distribution and durability. You will notice the difference immediately when lifting the skillet, especially when it is full of food.

The handle stays cool on the stovetop for short cooking tasks, but it heats up during longer cooking times and always gets hot in the oven. Use a dry towel or oven mitt when moving the skillet after the first few minutes of cooking.

## Maintenance overview

Cast iron requires a layer of polymerized oil called seasoning to prevent rust and create a natural nonstick surface. The skillet arrives pre-seasoned and ready to use. After cooking, wash it by hand with warm water and a small amount of soap if needed, then dry it completely and apply a thin coat of cooking oil. See our guide on cast iron seasoning and care for more detailed instructions on building and maintaining the seasoning layer over time.

Do not put the skillet in the dishwasher or leave it soaking in water, as both will strip the seasoning and promote rust.

## FAQ

**Can I use metal utensils in this skillet?** Yes. Cast iron is durable enough to handle metal spatulas, tongs, and spoons without scratching or damaging the cooking surface. Metal utensils will not harm the seasoning layer.

**Will the skillet work on my glass cooktop?** Yes, but lift it rather than sliding it across the surface to avoid scratching the glass. Cast iron's rough bottom texture can damage smooth cooktops if dragged.

**How do I know when the skillet is hot enough?** Flick a few drops of water onto the surface. If they sizzle and evaporate within 2–3 seconds, the skillet is ready for cooking. Cast iron takes longer to heat than nonstick but holds that heat consistently once it reaches temperature.
