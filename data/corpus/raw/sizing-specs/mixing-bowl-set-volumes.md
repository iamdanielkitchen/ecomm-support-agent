# Mixing bowl set volumes

The Ceramic Mixing Bowl Set (FG-KIT-003) includes three nested bowls with capacities of 1.5 quarts, 3 quarts, and 5 quarts. The smallest works well for whisking a few eggs or tossing a side salad, the medium handles cake batter or marinating chicken, and the largest is built for bread dough, large salads, or serving a crowd.

## Bowl dimensions and capacities

Each bowl is wheel-thrown stoneware with a glazed interior and matte exterior.

**Small bowl:** 1.5-quart capacity, approximately 7 inches in diameter and 3.5 inches tall. Use this for whisking two to four eggs, mixing vinaigrettes, or holding prepped ingredients like chopped herbs or nuts.

**Medium bowl:** 3-quart capacity, approximately 9 inches in diameter and 4.5 inches tall. This size handles pancake or muffin batter (roughly 12 to 18 muffins), marinating up to two pounds of protein, or tossing a salad for four to six people.

**Large bowl:** 5-quart capacity, approximately 11 inches in diameter and 5.5 inches tall. Ideal for bread dough (two to three loaves), mixing cookie dough for several dozen cookies, or serving pasta or grain salads at gatherings. The wide rim gives you room to fold ingredients without spilling.

All three bowls nest inside one another for compact storage.

## Typical uses by bowl size

Match the bowl to the task to avoid overflows and make mixing easier.

The **1.5-quart bowl** is your prep bowl. Crack eggs, whisk a small amount of cream, hold grated cheese, or mix a single serving of overnight oats. It is too small for stand-mixer attachments but works well with a hand whisk or fork.

The **3-quart bowl** bridges everyday baking and cooking. Mix batter for a standard 9x13 cake, toss a pound of roasted vegetables with olive oil and seasoning, or marinate chicken thighs. This is the most versatile size in the set.

The **5-quart bowl** handles volume. Knead pizza or sandwich dough, prepare a large batch of mashed potatoes, mix fruit salad for a potluck, or use it as a serving bowl for popcorn or chips. The tall sides contain splashes when you are folding or beating by hand.

## Care and cleaning

All three bowls are dishwasher-safe on the top rack. Hand washing extends the life of the glaze and prevents chipping from rack contact. See our guide on ceramic cookware and dishwasher use for more details on protecting stoneware finishes.

Avoid thermal shock by letting hot bowls cool before rinsing with cold water. The stoneware can chip if dropped on hard surfaces, so handle the nested set carefully when unstacking.

## FAQ

**Can I use these bowls in the microwave?** Yes, all three are microwave-safe. The stoneware heats evenly, but the bowls themselves will get hot, so use a towel or oven mitt when removing them.

**Do the bowls have pouring spouts?** No. The rims are smooth and rounded without spouts. Tilt and pour slowly, or use a spatula to transfer thick batters.

**Will the largest bowl fit under a stand mixer?** Most stand mixers accommodate a 5-quart bowl, but clearance depends on your mixer model. Measure the distance from your mixer head to the base before planning to use the large bowl with a beater attachment.
