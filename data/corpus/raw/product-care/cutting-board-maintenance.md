# Cutting board maintenance

Your walnut cutting board (FG-KIT-002) will last for years when you clean it correctly after each use and treat it with mineral oil once a month. Two mistakes crack hardwood faster than anything else: running it through the dishwasher and leaving it submerged in water. Follow the steps below to keep the board flat, smooth, and safe for food prep.

## Daily cleaning after use

Wash the board by hand with warm water and dish soap immediately after prep work. Use a sponge or soft brush to remove food particles from the grain. Rinse thoroughly under running water, then stand the board upright against a dish rack or lean it against the wall so air reaches both sides. The board should dry completely within two to three hours.

Never place the walnut board in the dishwasher. High heat and prolonged moisture exposure cause warping and deep cracks that cannot be repaired. Do not leave the board sitting in a sink full of water or submerged in any basin — even a few minutes of soaking weakens the wood fibers and pulls out the protective oil.

## Monthly mineral oil treatment

Once a month, apply food-grade mineral oil to restore moisture and prevent the wood from drying out. Pour about one tablespoon of oil onto the clean, dry board and spread it evenly across the surface with a lint-free cloth or paper towel. Work the oil into the grain using circular motions, then flip the board and coat the other side. Let the oil soak in for four to six hours or overnight.

Wipe away any excess oil that has not absorbed into the wood. The board is ready to use as soon as the surface feels dry to the touch. You can find food-grade mineral oil at most grocery or hardware stores. See our guide on long-term care for oiled wood for seasonal deep-conditioning steps.

## What to avoid

Two actions will damage your board beyond repair. First, never run it through the dishwasher — the combination of high water temperature, harsh detergent, and the drying cycle will crack the wood within a few wash cycles. Second, do not soak the board in water for any length of time. Even a brief soak draws moisture into the core and causes the wood to swell unevenly, leading to splits along the grain.

Avoid cutting directly on the board with serrated knives or cleavers, which gouge the surface and create grooves where bacteria can collect. Use the board for slicing vegetables, bread, and cheese. For raw meat, consider a separate plastic cutting board that you can sanitize at higher temperatures.

## FAQ

**How do I know when the board needs more oil?** 
If the surface looks dull or feels dry when you run your hand across it, apply mineral oil. A well-oiled board has a subtle sheen and feels slightly waxy, not rough or thirsty.

**Can I use olive oil or coconut oil instead of mineral oil?** 
No. Cooking oils turn rancid over time and leave a sticky residue that attracts dust. Food-grade mineral oil does not spoil and will not affect the smell or taste of food.

**The board has a small crack along one edge — can I still use it?** 
Small surface cracks are cosmetic and safe for prep work. If the crack runs through the full thickness of the board or feels unstable when you press on both sides, retire the board and contact us about a replacement.
