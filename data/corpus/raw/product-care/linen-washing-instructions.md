# Washing and drying linen

Linen softens with every wash and gets better over time. Wash your Linen Throw Blanket in cold or warm water with mild detergent, tumble dry on low to speed softening, and embrace the natural texture — no ironing required.

## First wash expectations

Linen feels crisp when new. The first wash unlocks its signature soft drape. Wash your blanket before first use in cold or warm water (up to 105°F) on a gentle cycle with a small amount of mild detergent. Avoid bleach and fabric softener, which coat the fibers and prevent natural softening.

You will notice the fabric relax and gain softness immediately. After three to five washes, linen reaches its full soft hand. This evolution is part of the material's appeal. See our guide on About our linen for more on how European flax behaves over years of use.

## Water temperature and drying method

Cold water (under 80°F) preserves fiber strength and minimizes shrinkage. Warm water (80–105°F) cleans effectively and accelerates softening. Hot water is not necessary and may cause slight shrinkage over time.

Tumble drying on low heat softens linen faster than air drying. The mechanical action in the dryer breaks down pectin between fibers, giving you that lived-in feel in fewer washes. Remove the blanket while still slightly damp to reduce wrinkles. Low heat is essential — high heat weakens flax fibers and shortens the blanket's lifespan.

Air drying takes longer but extends fiber life. Hang or lay flat in a shaded area. Linen dries quickly due to its hollow fiber structure. Air-dried linen will soften more gradually, reaching full drape after more washes than tumble-dried pieces.

## Handling wrinkles without ironing

Linen wrinkles naturally. This texture is intentional and part of the fabric's relaxed aesthetic. If you prefer a smoother look, remove the blanket from the dryer while damp and shake it out firmly. Fold or drape it immediately. Residual moisture releases most creases as the fabric dries.

For stubborn wrinkles, hang the blanket in a steamy bathroom for ten minutes or mist lightly with water and smooth by hand. Linen responds to moisture and weight. You do not need an iron. Over time, regular use and washing train the fabric to hold its shape with minimal creasing.

## Stain treatment and long-term care

Treat stains promptly by blotting (not rubbing) with cold water and a small amount of mild detergent. Work from the outside of the stain inward. Rinse thoroughly before washing. Linen is durable and stain-resistant when addressed quickly.

Wash your blanket every two to four weeks with regular use, or as needed. Store clean and completely dry in a breathable cotton bag or on an open shelf. Avoid plastic bins, which trap moisture and can cause mildew. Linen's antimicrobial properties make it naturally resistant to odor between washes.

## FAQ

**Can I wash linen with other fabrics?** Yes. Wash linen with similar colors and weights. Avoid washing with heavy items like denim that can snag the weave or with synthetics that pill and transfer lint.

**How much will my linen blanket shrink?** Expect 2–3% shrinkage after the first wash if you use warm water and tumble dry. Cold water and air drying minimize this. Shrinkage stabilizes after the first few washes.

**Does linen pill or fray over time?** Quality linen does not pill. Edges may soften slightly with use, which is normal. If you notice loose threads, trim them with scissors rather than pulling.
