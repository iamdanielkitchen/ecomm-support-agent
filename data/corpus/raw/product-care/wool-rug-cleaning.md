# Caring for wool rugs

Your Wool Area Rug, 5×7 (FG-HOM-002) stays soft and vibrant with regular light maintenance and prompt attention to spills. Weekly vacuuming and immediate blotting keep most wear at bay, while a professional cleaning every 12 to 18 months protects the fibers for years of use.

## Weekly maintenance

Vacuum your wool rug once or twice a week using a canister vacuum or an upright with the beater bar turned off. The beater bar can snag or fray wool fibers, especially along the edges. If your vacuum lacks a beater-bar shutoff, use the hose attachment with a soft-bristle floor tool.

Rotate the rug 180 degrees every three months so foot traffic wears the pile evenly. High-traffic areas develop a slight sheen over time—rotation distributes that patina across the surface.

Use a rug pad underneath to prevent slipping and to cushion the backing. The pad also allows air to circulate, which reduces moisture buildup and extends the rug's life.

## Treating spills and spots

Blot liquid spills immediately with a clean white cloth or paper towel. Press down firmly and lift—do not rub, which pushes the liquid deeper into the fibers. Work from the outer edge of the spill toward the center to keep it from spreading.

For water-based spills like coffee or juice, blot until no more liquid transfers to the cloth. If a stain remains, mix one teaspoon of mild dish soap with one cup of lukewarm water. Dampen a cloth with the solution, blot the stain gently, then blot again with a cloth dampened in plain water to rinse. Let the area air-dry completely.

For grease or oil, sprinkle cornstarch or baking soda over the spot and let it sit for 15 minutes to absorb the oil. Vacuum up the powder, then treat any residue with the soap solution described above.

Avoid enzyme cleaners, bleach, or steam cleaners. Wool is a protein fiber, and harsh chemicals or high heat can cause permanent damage or discoloration.

## Professional cleaning

Plan to have your rug professionally cleaned every 12 to 18 months, depending on household traffic and whether you have pets. Professional cleaners use pH-balanced solutions and controlled drying that protect wool's natural lanolin and prevent shrinkage.

Between cleanings, if a stain resists home treatment or if the rug develops an odor, contact a cleaner sooner. Waiting too long allows stains to set and odors to bond with the fibers.

## FAQ

**Can I wash my wool rug at home?**  
No. Wool rugs require controlled moisture and drying to avoid felting, shrinking, or color bleeding. Home washing machines and even bathtub soaking introduce too much water and agitation. Stick to spot cleaning and professional service.

**How do I remove pet hair from the rug?**  
Vacuum with the beater bar off, then use a rubber-bristle brush or a damp rubber glove to lift stubborn hair from the pile. The static from rubber grabs hair that suction alone misses.

**Will sunlight fade my rug?**  
Direct sunlight can fade natural dyes over time. Rotate the rug regularly and use window treatments during peak sun hours if the rug sits in a bright room. Fading happens gradually and evenly with rotation, so it remains less noticeable.
