# Ceramic and dishwasher use

Fieldstone ceramic pieces—including our Ceramic Mixing Bowl Set and Espresso Cups—are dishwasher-safe and built for daily use. The glaze finish resists staining and holds up to standard dishwasher cycles. The key care point is avoiding thermal shock: never move ceramic directly from the refrigerator into a hot oven or from a hot oven into cold water.

## Dishwasher-safe ceramic items

All glazed ceramic products in the Fieldstone catalog can go in the dishwasher. This includes the Ceramic Mixing Bowl Set (FG-KIT-003) and the Espresso Cups, Set of 4 (FG-KIT-004). Use the normal wash cycle. Avoid the high-heat drying setting if your pieces have metallic trim, though standard Fieldstone ceramics have no metal accents.

Top-rack placement works well for smaller items like espresso cups. Larger mixing bowls fit securely on the bottom rack. Make sure bowls are angled so water drains rather than pooling inside.

Hand-washing with warm soapy water is always fine and may extend the life of any decorative elements. Either method keeps the glaze intact.

## Preventing thermal shock

Ceramic can crack when exposed to rapid temperature changes. Do not place a cold mixing bowl straight into a preheated oven, and do not run a hot ceramic piece under cold tap water.

If you store ingredients in a ceramic bowl in the refrigerator and plan to bake with them, let the bowl sit at room temperature for fifteen to twenty minutes first. When removing a hot bowl from the oven, set it on a wooden cutting board or trivet—never directly on a cold countertop or in the sink.

Room-temperature ceramic can go into a preheated oven without issue. Just avoid the extremes in either direction.

## Caring for the glaze

The glaze on Fieldstone ceramic resists most stains, but deep pigments from beets or turmeric may leave faint marks over time. A paste of baking soda and water, applied with a soft sponge, lifts most discoloration. Let the paste sit for five minutes before rinsing.

Avoid abrasive scouring pads or steel wool. These scratch the glaze and create tiny channels where stains can settle. A nylon scrub brush handles baked-on food without damaging the surface.

## FAQ

**Can I microwave Fieldstone ceramic bowls?**  
Yes. Ceramic mixing bowls and espresso cups are microwave-safe as long as they have no metallic decoration. Heat liquids or food in short intervals and use oven mitts when removing hot items.

**What if a bowl develops a hairline crack?**  
Stop using it for food. A cracked bowl can harbor bacteria and may shatter under heat. Ceramic pieces with structural damage cannot be repaired safely.

**Do I need to season ceramic like cast iron?**  
No. Glazed ceramic does not require seasoning. Wash it before first use and you are ready to cook. The glaze provides a naturally non-stick surface that stays intact with normal cleaning.
