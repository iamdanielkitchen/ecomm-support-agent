# Candle safety and burn tips

Our Holiday Candle (FG-SAL-001) is hand-poured with a soy-blend wax and designed to burn cleanly for 40–50 hours. Following a few simple practices during the first burn and throughout the candle's life will prevent tunneling, extend burn time, and keep the flame safe and steady.

## First burn matters most

The first time you light the candle sets the wax memory. Let the melt pool reach the edge of the container before extinguishing — typically 2 to 3 hours for our 3.5-inch diameter vessel. If you blow out the flame early, the wax will tunnel on every subsequent burn, wasting product and shortening the candle's life. Plan your first session when you can monitor the candle without interruption.

Never burn for more than 4 hours at a time. Longer sessions overheat the container and can cause the wick to mushroom or the flame to flicker unevenly.

## Wick trimming and maintenance

Before each burn, trim the wick to 1/4 inch. A wick that is too long produces soot, an unstable flame, and black smoke. Use scissors or a dedicated wick trimmer, and discard any charred debris that falls into the wax.

If the flame grows taller than 1 inch or flickers excessively, extinguish the candle, let it cool, trim the wick, and relight. A steady flame about 1/2 to 3/4 inch tall indicates proper burn conditions.

## Safe burning environment

Place the candle on a heat-resistant, stable surface away from drafts, vents, and flammable materials. Keep it out of reach of children and pets. Never leave a burning candle unattended, and extinguish it before leaving the room or going to sleep.

Stop burning when 1/2 inch of wax remains at the bottom. Continuing past this point can overheat the container and create a fire hazard.

## FAQ

**Can I burn the candle in a small bathroom or bedroom?**  
Yes, as long as the room is ventilated and the candle sits on a stable, heat-resistant surface. Avoid placing it near shower steam or directly under air vents.

**What if the candle develops an uneven surface after a few burns?**  
This is normal. As long as the first burn created a full melt pool, minor surface irregularities will not affect performance. Trim the wick and continue burning as usual.

**How do I clean soot from the container?**  
Let the candle cool completely, then wipe the interior glass with a damp cloth. Persistent soot indicates the wick was too long — trim it before the next burn.
