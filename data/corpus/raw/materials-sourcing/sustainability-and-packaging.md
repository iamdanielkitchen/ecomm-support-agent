# Sustainability and packaging

Fieldstone Goods uses 100% recycled corrugate for shipping boxes and chooses minimal packaging to reduce waste. We do not include paper filler in most shipments because cushioning adds transport weight and emissions without protecting products that already arrive wrapped. Expedited shipping carries a higher carbon cost per item due to less efficient routing and partial truck loads.

## What our shipping boxes are made from

Every box that leaves our warehouse is made from post-consumer recycled corrugate. The outer brown layer comes from previously used cardboard that has been pulped and reformed. Box strength meets industry standards for parcel shipment, and the material itself can be recycled again in your curbside bin after use.

We print shipping labels with soy-based ink and use water-activated tape, which bonds directly to the corrugate and does not interfere with the recycling stream. Plastic packing tape would need to be removed before the box could be processed.

## Why we skip the filler paper

Many retailers add crinkle paper, shredded newsprint, or kraft cushioning to fill empty space in a box. Fieldstone does not include these materials in standard shipments. Each item in the catalog arrives wrapped in its original protective sleeve or bag, which provides enough cushioning for transit. Adding loose fill increases the package's dimensional weight, which in turn increases fuel consumption per delivery and raises the shipment's overall carbon footprint.

If an item is fragile or irregularly shaped, we use only the amount of corrugated insert or wrap needed to prevent shifting. We do not add filler for aesthetic reasons or to create an unboxing experience.

## Carbon trade-offs in expedited shipping

Standard ground shipping consolidates packages into full truckloads that travel along optimized routes. This method uses less fuel per item and produces fewer emissions. Expedited shipping requires partial loads, priority aircraft cargo, and hub-and-spoke networks that move smaller batches more frequently. The result is a higher carbon cost per package.

We offer expedited delivery for time-sensitive orders, but standard shipping remains the lower-impact choice when delivery speed is flexible. See our guide on Shipping methods and timelines for transit windows and cutoff times.

## Material sourcing across the catalog

Packaging choices are one part of a larger effort to source responsibly. Wool products in the catalog come from certified farms that meet animal welfare and land stewardship standards. See Wool sourcing and certifications for details on what those standards require and how suppliers are audited.

Linen, ceramic, and wood items are made by workshops that prioritize natural materials and minimal chemical finishing. We list the origin country and primary material for each product on its catalog page.

## FAQ

**Can I request plastic-free packaging?**  
All standard shipments are already plastic-free except for the protective sleeve around wool and linen items, which prevents moisture damage in transit. We cannot remove that sleeve without risking product loss.

**What should I do with the box after delivery?**  
Break down the box flat and place it in your curbside recycling bin. Remove the shipping label if your hauler requires it, though most facilities screen out paper labels during processing.

**Does expedited shipping use different packaging?**  
No. Expedited and standard orders use the same recycled corrugate boxes. The only difference is routing speed and transport method.
