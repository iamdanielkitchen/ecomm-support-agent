# About our linen

Our linen comes from European flax and is woven at a fourth-generation mill in Lithuania. We chose this supply chain because European linen offers proven durability, a naturally textured hand, and environmental advantages over cotton or synthetic fibers. Every linen item in our catalog—including the Linen Throw Blanket (FG-HOM-001)—is made from this same yarn stock.

## Why European flax

Flax thrives in the temperate, rainy climates of Belgium, France, and the Netherlands, where it needs no irrigation. The crop also requires minimal pesticides and naturally improves soil health between rotations. After harvest, flax stalks are laid in fields to ret—a natural process in which dew and microbes break down the outer stalk, leaving long bast fibers that spinners twist into linen yarn. European growers have refined this technique over centuries, and the result is fiber that is strong, absorbent, and naturally lustrous.

Linen from flax grown in humid maritime climates tends to be finer and more uniform than flax from hotter, drier regions. This matters for items you will wash dozens or hundreds of times. European linen softens with age while maintaining tensile strength, so a blanket that feels crisp when new becomes supple after six months of regular laundering.

## Our Lithuanian mill partner

We work with a family-owned weaving mill outside Vilnius that has specialized in linen since 1952. The mill sources flax exclusively from cooperatives in Belgium and northern France, spins it into yarn on-site, and weaves it on Jacquard looms that allow precise control over weight and drape. Our throw blanket uses a medium-weight plain weave—sturdy enough for daily use, breathable enough for year-round comfort.

The mill runs quality checks at three stages: raw fiber grading, post-spin yarn testing, and finished fabric inspection. Each bolt is examined for slubs, breaks, and color consistency before it ships to our manufacturing partners. This level of oversight reduces the risk of defects and ensures that every piece of linen we sell meets the same standard.

## What to expect from linen goods

Linen starts with a slightly crisp, textured feel and softens noticeably after the first few washes. It wrinkles easily, which is part of its character—if you prefer a smooth look, iron while slightly damp. Linen is also naturally temperature-regulating. The hollow core of each flax fiber wicks moisture away from skin in summer and traps warmth in winter, making it ideal for throw blankets and bedding.

Linen may shrink 3–5% in the first wash. For guidance on washing and drying, see our guide on linen washing instructions. For measurements before and after expected shrinkage, check our article on throw blanket dimensions.

## FAQ

**Does linen pill or wear thin over time?**  
High-quality European linen resists pilling because the long bast fibers lie parallel in the yarn. You may see slight surface fuzz after the first wash, but this disappears as the fabric softens. Linen typically outlasts cotton by a factor of two or three in daily-use items.

**Why does linen cost more than cotton?**  
Flax yields less fiber per acre than cotton, and the retting and spinning processes are more labor-intensive. European linen also carries higher labor and environmental standards than commodity textiles, which adds to the base cost. The tradeoff is longevity—linen goods often last a decade or more with regular use.
