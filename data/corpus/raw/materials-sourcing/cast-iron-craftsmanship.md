# How our cast iron is made

The Cast Iron Skillet, 10" (FG-KIT-001) is sand-cast in a Wisconsin foundry using molten iron poured into hand-packed molds. This traditional method creates a heavier, more durable pan than stamped or machined alternatives. Each skillet is pre-seasoned with organic flaxseed oil before it ships, so it arrives ready to cook.

## Sand casting vs. machined iron

Sand casting means liquid iron is poured into a sand mold shaped around a pattern. Once the iron cools, the mold is broken away and the skillet is cleaned and finished. This process produces a thicker wall and base — our 10" skillet weighs just over 5 pounds, compared to 3 or 4 pounds for typical mass-market pans. That extra heft holds heat more evenly and reduces hot spots during searing or frying.

Machined cast iron starts with a flat iron blank that is spun or stamped into shape. It is lighter and faster to produce, but the thinner walls lose heat more quickly and can warp over high heat. Sand-cast iron stays stable across gas, electric, and induction stovetops.

## Why the skillet is heavier

The foundry uses a silica sand blend that allows for thicker walls without weakening the mold. A thicker base stores more thermal energy, which means the pan recovers temperature faster when cold food hits the surface. This is especially useful for browning proteins or cooking multiple batches of cornbread or pancakes.

The trade-off is weight. A 5-pound skillet requires a firm grip and two hands when transferring from stovetop to oven. If you are looking for capacity details or cooking tips, see our Cast Iron Capacity Guide.

## Pre-seasoning at the foundry

After the raw casting is cleaned, it goes through a three-layer seasoning process. Each skillet is coated with organic flaxseed oil and baked at 500°F. The oil polymerizes into a hard, dark patina that protects the iron from rust and provides a naturally nonstick surface. This takes place before the skillet leaves the foundry.

The factory seasoning is durable enough for immediate cooking, but it will continue to improve with use. Fats from cooking build up over time and darken the finish. For step-by-step care instructions, see our guide on Cast Iron Seasoning and Care.

## FAQ

**Does sand-cast iron need special cleaning?**  
No. Wash with hot water and a stiff brush. Avoid soap if possible, though a small amount will not strip the seasoning. Dry immediately and rub with a thin layer of oil after each use.

**Can I use the skillet on induction?**  
Yes. Cast iron works on gas, electric, and induction cooktops. The thick base heats slowly but holds temperature well once hot.

**Will the seasoning wear off?**  
The factory seasoning is permanent unless you strip it with harsh chemicals or leave the pan wet for days. Normal cooking and cleaning will not remove it. Light rust can be scrubbed off and re-seasoned at home.
