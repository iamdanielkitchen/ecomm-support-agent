# Where our wood comes from

The walnut in our Walnut Cutting Board comes from responsibly managed forests in the Appalachian region of the United States. Every board is milled from FSC-certified lumber, edge-glued, sanded to a food-safe finish, and treated with mineral oil before it ships. The price reflects both the quality of the raw material and the craft required to turn rough lumber into a durable kitchen tool.

## The walnut source

We source black walnut (Juglans nigra) from FSC-certified sawmills in Pennsylvania, West Virginia, and Ohio. FSC certification guarantees that the wood comes from forests managed for biodiversity, minimal ecological impact, and fair labor practices. Each board's lumber is kiln-dried to 6–8% moisture content to prevent warping and cracking once the board enters your kitchen.

Black walnut is prized for its rich color, tight grain, and natural resistance to bacteria. The boards vary slightly in tone — some lean darker chocolate, others show lighter sapwood streaks — because every tree is unique.

## From rough lumber to finished board

A cutting board is not simply a slice of log. The manufacturing process involves several steps:

- **Selection and grading**: Only premium-grade planks free of knots, splits, and sapwood inclusions make it into production.
- **Edge gluing**: Narrow strips are glued side by side with food-safe adhesive to create a stable, wide surface. Edge grain construction resists knife marks better than face grain.
- **Milling and sanding**: The glued slab is planed flat, cut to dimension, and sanded through progressively finer grits until smooth.
- **Oiling**: Each board receives multiple coats of USP-grade mineral oil to seal the wood and bring out its natural color.

This process takes about two weeks per production batch. See our guide on cutting board maintenance for tips on keeping the finish in good condition.

## What the price reflects

The $38.00 price of the Walnut Cutting Board accounts for certified raw material, skilled labor, and quality control. FSC-certified walnut costs 20–30% more than conventional lumber. Edge-glued construction requires precision joinery. Each board is inspected for flatness, finish consistency, and structural integrity before it ships.

You are paying for a tool that will last decades if properly maintained. For long-term care instructions, including how to re-oil the surface as it ages, see our guide on oiled wood long-term care.

## FAQ

**Does the board come with documentation of FSC certification?**  
The board itself does not ship with a certificate, but our supplier chain-of-custody records are available on request. Contact us with your order number if you need documentation for a project or institution.

**Why does my board look different from the one in the product photo?**  
Natural wood varies. Some boards have darker heartwood throughout, others show lighter sapwood edges or subtle mineral streaks. All variations meet our grading standards and perform identically in the kitchen.
