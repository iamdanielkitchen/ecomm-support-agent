# Tracking your order

Your tracking link arrives by email within one business day of order placement. Check your shipment's location, see what each status means, and know when to reach out if tracking stops updating.

## Finding your tracking link

You'll receive a shipping confirmation email from Fieldstone Goods once your order leaves the warehouse. This email contains your tracking number and a direct link to the carrier's tracking page. The subject line will read "Your Fieldstone order has shipped." If you don't see it in your inbox, check your spam or promotions folder—some email providers redirect order confirmations automatically.

The tracking number is also embedded in the link itself, so you can copy it and paste it directly into the carrier's website if the link doesn't work for any reason. Your shipping confirmation email also restates your estimated delivery window based on the shipping method you selected at checkout. See our guide on shipping methods and delivery timelines for details on standard versus expedited options.

## Understanding tracking statuses

Carrier tracking systems use a handful of common statuses. Here's what each one means:

**Label created.** The warehouse has printed a shipping label and the carrier has received the package details electronically. The physical package is waiting for pickup. This status typically lasts a few hours.

**In transit.** The carrier has the package and it's moving through their distribution network. Most standard shipments show this status for two to four days.

**Out for delivery.** The package is on a truck and will arrive by end of business day. Delivery times vary by route—residential deliveries often arrive between 9 a.m. and 8 p.m.

**Delivered.** The carrier has left the package at your address. If you didn't receive it, check common drop-off spots like a porch, mailroom, or with a building manager. For persistent issues, our article on delivery issues and lost packages walks through next steps.

**Exception.** The carrier encountered a delay—weather, incorrect address, access issues. Check the tracking page for notes. Most exceptions resolve within 24 hours, and the package resumes normal transit.

## What to do when tracking stalls

If your tracking hasn't updated in more than 48 hours and the package is still marked "in transit," the carrier may be experiencing a backlog or a scanning issue. This happens occasionally, especially during peak seasons.

Wait one additional business day. Many packages arrive on time even when tracking lags. If the package still hasn't moved after 72 hours total, or if you're past your estimated delivery date, contact Ember, our support agent. Have your order number (format FG-NNNNNN) and the email address you used at checkout ready. Ember can verify delivery status with the carrier and, if the package is confirmed lost, start the replacement or refund process.

Tracking sometimes updates late in the evening or overnight, so refresh the page the following morning before escalating.

## FAQ

**Can I change the delivery address after the order ships?**  
No. Once the carrier has the package, the address cannot be changed. If you need the order sent elsewhere, you can refuse delivery when it arrives, and it will return to Fieldstone. Contact Ember to arrange a refund or reshipment to the correct address once the return is processed.

**Why does tracking show "delivered" but I don't have the package?**  
Check with neighbors, your building's front desk, or any household members who may have brought it inside. Carriers sometimes mark delivery a few hours early. If 24 hours pass and the package doesn't appear, reach out to Ember with your order number—we'll file a claim with the carrier.

**Does Fieldstone use multiple carriers?**  
We work with a primary national carrier for most shipments. Your shipping confirmation email identifies which carrier is handling your order. Tracking behavior and delivery windows can vary slightly depending on the carrier and your location.
