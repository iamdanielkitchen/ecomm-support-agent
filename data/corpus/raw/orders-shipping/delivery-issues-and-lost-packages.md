# Delivery issues and lost packages

If your tracking shows delivered but you don't have your package, or if your order seems stuck in transit, start by waiting 48 hours. Carriers sometimes scan packages early or deliver to a secure location you haven't checked yet. Most packages appear within two days of a "delivered" scan.

## When tracking shows delivered but you don't have the package

Check these spots first:

- **Front porch, back porch, or side entrance.** Drivers often leave packages where they're least visible from the street.
- **Mailroom, leasing office, or building concierge.** Apartment and condo deliveries are frequently signed for by staff.
- **Behind planters, furniture, or inside a screen door.** Drivers tuck packages out of sight for security.
- **With a neighbor.** Some carriers deliver to an adjacent address if yours seems inaccessible.

Confirm the delivery address on your order confirmation email matches where you expected the package. If you recently moved or entered an old address at checkout, the package may have gone to the wrong location.

Wait 48 hours from the "delivered" timestamp. Scans occasionally run ahead of the actual drop-off, especially late in the day. See our guide on Tracking your order for more on how to read tracking events.

## When your package is delayed in transit

Shipments sometimes pause at regional hubs during weather events, peak volume periods, or carrier staffing issues. If tracking hasn't updated in three or more business days, the delay is likely on the carrier's side.

Standard shipping takes 3–5 business days from the ship date. Expedited shipping takes 1–2 business days. Count only business days — weekends and federal holidays don't apply.

Check the latest scan location in your tracking details. If the package is still moving between facilities, it's in the carrier network and should resume normal transit soon.

## When to contact support

Reach out to our team if:

- 48 hours have passed since a "delivered" scan and you've checked all possible locations
- Tracking shows no movement for five or more business days
- The package was delivered to the wrong address and you cannot retrieve it
- Tracking indicates the package is lost or damaged in transit

When you contact us, have your order number (format FG-NNNNNN) and the email address used at checkout ready. We'll open a claim with the carrier and work toward a resolution, which may include a replacement shipment or refund depending on the situation.

You can reach support through the contact form on our site — see Contacting customer support for details.

## If the package was stolen

If you confirmed delivery to the correct address but believe the package was stolen, file a report with your local police department and provide us with the report number when you contact support. We'll use that information as part of the carrier claim process.

Some home insurance or renters insurance policies cover stolen deliveries — check your policy if the order value is significant.

## FAQ

**Can I reroute a package that's already shipped?**  
No. Once the carrier picks up your order, we cannot change the delivery address or hold it at a facility. If you need to intercept a shipment, contact the carrier directly using the tracking number, though they may charge a fee and success is not guaranteed.

**What if only part of my order arrived?**  
Larger orders sometimes ship in multiple boxes with separate tracking numbers. Check your shipping confirmation email for all tracking links. If you've received all tracked packages and items are still missing, contact support with your order number and a list of what didn't arrive.

**Does Fieldstone replace lost packages?**  
We work with the carrier to investigate and resolve delivery issues. Replacement or refund depends on the outcome of the carrier claim and the specific circumstances. We'll keep you updated throughout the process.
